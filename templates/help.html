Help & Settings Guide

Motion Detection

Threshold
Range: 1–100
How different a pixel must be between frames before it's counted as motion. Lower values make the camera more sensitive — it will trigger on subtle movements like shadows or leaves. Higher values only trigger on obvious movement like a bird landing. Start around 20–30 and adjust if you're getting too many false triggers or missing birds.
Min Pixels
Range: 50–5000
The minimum number of changed pixels required to trigger a capture. This filters out tiny disturbances like a single leaf blowing or a flickering shadow. If a bird is small in the frame, lower this value. If you're getting captures triggered by wind or lighting changes, raise it. 500–1000 works well for a bird at a typical feeder distance.
Cooldown
Range: 1–30 seconds
After a motion capture is saved, how many seconds to wait before capturing again. This prevents filling your storage with hundreds of nearly identical frames of the same bird sitting still. Set it low (1–3s) if you want to catch every moment of a bird's visit, or high (10–30s) to only capture one image per visit and save storage space.
Min Confidence %
Range: 0–100%
The minimum confidence score the bird classifier must reach before a sighting is logged. Motion captures are always saved regardless of this setting — this only controls what appears in your Sightings feed and Stats. Set it lower (20–40%) to catch more species including uncertain detections, or higher (60–80%) if you only want high-confidence identifications in your log.
Tip: tuning for your feeder. If you're getting too many false captures, raise Threshold and Min Pixels first. If you're missing birds, lower Threshold. The Cooldown is the most effective way to control storage usage — a 5 second cooldown means a bird that stays 30 seconds generates at most 6 captures instead of hundreds.

Camera

Brightness
Range: -1.0 – 1.0
Offsets the overall exposure. Use a small positive value if the image looks dark, or a negative value if it's washed out. For outdoor daylight use, 0.0 (default) usually works well — the camera's auto-exposure handles most conditions.
Contrast
Range: 0.0 – 8.0
Controls the difference between dark and light areas. Higher values make colours pop more but can lose detail in shadows and highlights. 1.0 is neutral. Values around 1.2–1.5 work well for outdoor bird photography.
Saturation
Range: 0.0 – 4.0
Controls colour intensity. 1.0 is neutral. Raise it slightly (1.2–1.5) to make bird plumage colours more vivid. Set to 0.0 for black-and-white output.
Sharpness
Range: 0.0 – 8.0
Controls edge enhancement. 1.0 is neutral. A value of 1.5–2.0 can help bring out feather detail. Too high will introduce noise and haloing around edges.

Focus

Mode
Auto / Manual
Auto lets the camera refocus on its own, which is fine most of the time. Manual locks focus at a fixed distance and stops the lens hunting — useful when your feeder sits at a known spot and autofocus keeps drifting off to the trees behind it.
Position
Manual mode only
The fixed focus distance, in diopters (1 / metres). 0 is infinity, 1 is roughly 1 m, 2 is about 0.5 m, 4 is about 0.25 m. Nudge it until the feeder looks sharp in the live view.

Timelapse

Interval
Range: 0–60 minutes
How often to automatically save a timelapse frame. Set to 0 to disable. A 5-minute interval over a full day produces 288 frames — enough for a smooth timelapse video. Use the Gallery page to build the video once you've collected frames.

Fan (Recording)

Mode during slow-mo
Normal / Quiet / Silent
Controls the cooling fan while a slow-mo clip records, so its high-pitched whine doesn't end up in the audio if you've wired in a microphone. Normal leaves the fan on its usual automatic thermal control. Quiet drops it to a low steady speed — a soft hum instead of a sudden spin-up. Silent switches it off for the few seconds of the burst, then the system turns it straight back on. The processor is always protected by its own firmware temperature limits, and a watchdog restores cooling early if things warm up mid-recording.
Only matters if you record audio. Slow-mo bursts last a few seconds, so even Silent barely moves the temperature. No microphone attached? Leave it on Normal.

Animals (Non-Bird)

Capture
On / Off
Keep photos of non-bird animals the detector spots — squirrels, deer, foxes, raccoons, cats, and the like. They're tracked separately from your bird sightings, so they never muddy the bird stats. Turn off if you only care about birds.
Slow-mo record
On / Off
Also grab a slow-motion clip when an animal shows up, the same way a bird triggers one. Turn off if you just want stills of passing critters.
Notify
On / Off
Send a phone notification when an animal is detected (needs a notification URL configured). Off by default — a busy yard can get chatty.
Daytime only. Animal detection runs on the same daylight frames as bird detection. There's no night vision, so nocturnal visitors slip by after dark.
